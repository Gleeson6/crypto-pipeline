# Miner Flows & Macro Correlation — Bitcoin Price Signals

## Part 1: Miner Flow Signals

Miners are Bitcoin's largest structural sellers — they receive BTC as block
rewards and must periodically sell to cover operational costs (electricity,
hardware). Tracking when miners sell vs. hold reveals structural supply pressure.

---

### Miner Outflow to Exchanges

**Definition:** BTC moving from known miner wallets to exchange wallets.

**Why it matters:** When miners send coins to exchanges, they are likely
preparing to sell. Large miner outflows signal incoming supply pressure.

**Signal patterns:**

| Miner Outflow | Context | Signal |
|---------------|---------|--------|
| Spike (> 2× 30-day avg) | Price at local high | Strong bearish — distribution |
| Spike | Price in downtrend | Capitulation — potential bottom signal |
| Consistently low | Any | Miners HODLing — bullish supply signal |
| Post-halving (first 3-6mo) | Reduced block reward | Miners financially stressed, watch outflows |

**Miner capitulation:**
When price drops significantly below miner cost of production (hash price),
unprofitable miners shut down and sell reserves to cover costs. This often
marks the final stage of a bear market. Signs:
- Hash rate declining (miners shutting off)
- Miner outflows spiking despite low price
- Miner reserve at multi-year lows
→ Historically one of the best long-term accumulation signals

### Miner Reserve

**Definition:** Total BTC held in known miner wallets.

- **Declining reserve:** Miners selling consistently → structural supply pressure
- **Stable/rising reserve:** Miners accumulating → confident in higher future prices

### Hash Rate as a Proxy Signal

**Hash rate** = total computational power securing the Bitcoin network.

- **Rising hash rate:** More miners profitable and adding hardware → bullish
  long-term signal (miners invest in the future)
- **Declining hash rate:** Miners unprofitable, shutting down → bearish /
  capitulation signal
- **Hash rate recovery after decline:** New miners entering at lower cost basis
  → bullish structural signal

**For 1–4H swings:** Miner flow data is slow-moving (daily). Use as a
background filter:
- Miner outflow spike → don't add to longs, reduce exposure
- Miner outflow low + hash rate stable/rising → context supports longs

## Part 2: Macro Correlation Signals

Bitcoin does not move in isolation. Since institutional adoption (2020+),
Bitcoin has shown significant correlation with macro risk assets.

---

### U.S. Federal Reserve & Interest Rates

**Mechanism:**
Higher interest rates → stronger USD → higher opportunity cost for non-yielding
assets (gold, Bitcoin) → reduced risk appetite → downward pressure on Bitcoin.

Lower interest rates (or rate cut expectations) → weaker USD → capital flows
into risk assets → upward pressure on Bitcoin.

**Historical patterns:**
- 2022 Fed rate hike cycle (0% → 5.25%): Bitcoin dropped ~75% from peak
- 2020 near-zero rates: Bitcoin ran from $10K to $69K
- Rate cut expectations in late 2023: Bitcoin began recovery to new ATH

**Signal interpretation:**
| Fed Action | Bitcoin Impact | Timeframe |
|------------|---------------|-----------|
| Rate hike (surprise) | Immediate sell-off | Hours–days |
| Rate hike (priced in) | Muted, "sell the news" already happened | Days |
| Rate cut (surprise) | Strong rally | Hours–days |
| Pause / pivot signal | Relief rally | Days–weeks |
| Hawkish Fed minutes | Gradual decline | Days |
| Dovish Fed minutes | Gradual rally | Days |

**Key dates to watch:** FOMC meeting dates (8× per year), CPI release,
PCE inflation data, Fed Chair press conferences.

**For 1–4H swings:** On FOMC days, volatility spikes — widen stops or
stay out. The first 30–60 minutes after the rate decision are typically
the most volatile and unreliable for directional trading.

---

### DXY (U.S. Dollar Index)

**Definition:** Measures the USD against a basket of 6 major currencies.

**Correlation with Bitcoin:** Generally inverse — strong dollar = weak Bitcoin,
weak dollar = strong Bitcoin. Not perfect but historically significant.

```
DXY rising → USD strengthening → Bitcoin headwind
DXY falling → USD weakening → Bitcoin tailwind
DXY at multi-year high → historically strong long-term Bitcoin buy signal
DXY at multi-year low → historically favors continued Bitcoin strength
```

**For 1–4H swings:** Check DXY trend direction as a macro filter:
- DXY in clear downtrend → macro tailwind for longs
- DXY in clear uptrend → macro headwind, be more conservative with longs

---

### S&P 500 / Risk Asset Correlation

**Bitcoin–S&P 500 correlation** has averaged 0.3–0.6 since 2020 (institutional
adoption era). It is highest during:
- Risk-off events (market crashes, geopolitical events)
- Liquidity crises (crypto and equities sell off together)

It is lowest during:
- Bitcoin-specific catalysts (halving, ETF approval, regulatory news)
- Crypto-specific sell-offs unrelated to macro

**Signal patterns:**
- S&P 500 in sharp drawdown → expect Bitcoin to correlate downward
- S&P 500 making new ATH + Bitcoin lagging → potential catch-up trade
- Bitcoin rallying while S&P 500 falling → Bitcoin decoupling, strong signal

---

### Gold Correlation

Bitcoin is sometimes called "digital gold" — a store-of-value / inflation hedge.

- **Gold rallying + Bitcoin lagging:** Potential Bitcoin catch-up trade
- **Both rallying:** Macro environment favoring real assets / inflation hedges
- **Gold falling + Bitcoin rallying:** Bitcoin is behaving as risk-on, not
  defensive — adjust macro interpretation accordingly

---

## Combining Macro + On-Chain for Swing Trades

**High-conviction long (macro + on-chain aligned):**
1. DXY in downtrend (macro tailwind)
2. Fed in pause or cut mode (risk-on environment)
3. Exchange outflows (on-chain bullish)
4. Funding rate neutral or negative (not over-leveraged)
5. MVRV 1.0–2.5 (not in distribution territory)
→ Highest-probability long setup. Scale in aggressively.

**High-conviction short / avoid longs:**
1. DXY in uptrend (macro headwind)
2. Fed actively hiking (risk-off)
3. Exchange inflow spike (on-chain bearish)
4. Funding rate highly positive (over-leveraged longs)
5. MVRV > 3.0 (distribution territory)
→ Exit longs, reduce exposure, consider shorts with tight stops.
